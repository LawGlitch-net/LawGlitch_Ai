Our Manifesto

The Future of AI
That Gets Things Done.

We didn't set out to build another AI company. We set out to fix what the industry got wrong.

"The measure of intelligence is not the quality of answers — it's the quality of actions taken."
01

The Problem

The AI industry promised transformation but delivered conversation. Billions spent on models that generate text but can't execute a single real-world task without human hand-holding.

02

Our Response

We built LawGlitch — autonomous intelligence that lives in your browser, understands your context, and acts on your behalf. Not another chatbot. A worker.

03

The Mission

To build the future of AI that gets things done. Where every professional has access to an intelligent copilot that researches, automates, captures, and synthesizes — continuously, privately, and at scale.

04

The Vision

A world where AI doesn't replace human judgment — it amplifies it. Where the barrier between intent and execution is zero. Where intelligence is infrastructure, not a product you prompt.

What We Believe

These aren't marketing lines. They're engineering constraints.

AI should execute, not converse.

The industry built trillion-dollar oracles that answer questions. We're building workers that complete tasks.

Real-time intelligence over static knowledge.

Training data is history. The web is alive. Our systems crawl, verify, and synthesize live data.

Efficiency is the product.

Tiny, tuned models. Lower cost. Faster results. We don't throw compute at problems — we engineer precision.

User sovereignty is non-negotiable.

Every data capture is user-gated. Every AI instance is isolated. Every session is private.

Transparency over black boxes.

You should see what your AI sees, know what it does, and control when it acts. Full visibility.

Infrastructure, not features.

We don't build chatbots with extra buttons. We build autonomous intelligence infrastructure.

Our Promise

We will never ship AI that just talks. Every feature we build must pass one test:

"Does this help someone do something they couldn't do before?"

If the answer is no, it doesn't ship. We build autonomous intelligence infrastructure for the post-LLM era — where AI is measured by output, not conversation.
